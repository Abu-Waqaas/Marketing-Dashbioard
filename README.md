# Marketing-Dashboard
## 1. Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate the overall marketing performance of the business by examining key metrics such as customer acquisition, view counts, campaign effectiveness, and market share. The analysis aims to uncover patterns and trends that can guide future marketing decisions and strategy.


## 2. Goal

The primary goal is to:

- Understand how marketing campaigns drive customer acquisition and engagement over time
- Identify the most effective marketing campaign channels
- Assess the business's market position relative to competitors
- Provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention and acquisition rates


## 3. Data Preparation

## 🛠 Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning, Analysis & Dashboard)

The dataset used for this analysis included the following key fields:

- **Monthly View Counts** — tracking website/platform traffic across all 12 months
- **Customer Records** — total number of customers (50,550) used to compute acquisition metrics
- **Marketing Campaign Modes** — categorized by channel: Billpost, Facebook Ads, Google Ads, Instagram Ads, Referral, and Twitter Ads
- **Market Share Data** — segmented into total market share vs. competitor share
- **Sales Projections** — used to compute average projected sales figures

Data was cleaned, aggregated, and visualized in Excel. Key calculated metrics include:

- **Average Customer Retention Rate (CRR):** 79%
- **Average Customer Acquisition Rate (CAR):** 23 (avg)
- **Average Sales Projected:** 940,833.33
- **Total View Count:** 2,055,000

### 📈 View Count Trends
Monthly views fluctuated throughout the year, peaking at **1,300,000 in July** and dipping to a low of **650,000 in September**. This suggests strong mid-year engagement, possibly driven by seasonal campaigns, followed by a notable drop in Q3 that warrants further investigation.

### 👥 Top Customer Months
The top 6 months by customer count were **April (5,500), May (5,300), September (5,000), October (4,700), February (4,300), and June (4,300)**. April and May stand out as peak acquisition periods, which may align with targeted spring campaigns.

### 📣 Marketing Campaign Performance
- **Referral** was the most used campaign mode with **31 campaigns**, indicating strong word-of-mouth or partner-driven marketing.
- **Twitter Ads** followed with **23**, and **Billpost** with **22**.
- **Google Ads** had the fewest campaigns at **13**, suggesting either cost constraints or lower prioritization.

### 🥧 Market Share
The business commands **91% of the total market share**, with competitors holding only **9%**. This dominant position reflects a strong market presence but also highlights the importance of maintaining customer satisfaction to protect market leadership.

### 🔄 Customer Retention vs. Acquisition
- **Average CRR of 79%** indicates that the majority of customers are being retained, which is a positive indicator of customer satisfaction and loyalty.
- **Average CAR of 23** (19.67 avg) suggests moderate new customer acquisition, pointing to room for growth in attracting new audiences.


## 5. Recommendations

1. **Address the September–October View Drop** — Investigate why views declined sharply in September (from 1.3M to 650K) and consider launching targeted re-engagement campaigns during Q3 to sustain momentum.

2. **Scale Up Referral Programs** — Since Referral is the top campaign mode, investing more into structured referral incentives (e.g., discounts, loyalty rewards) could amplify this channel further.

3. **Leverage Peak Months (April & May)** — These months consistently yield the highest customer counts. Pre-planned campaigns and offers timed to these periods should be a priority in the marketing calendar.

4. **Invest More in Google Ads** — With only 13 campaigns, Google Ads is underutilized. Given its targeting capabilities, increasing investment here could significantly boost acquisition rates.

5. **Improve CAR Without Compromising CRR** — The 79% retention rate is healthy, but the acquisition rate has room to grow. A balanced strategy that introduces new audience segments while nurturing existing customers will drive sustainable growth.

6. **Protect Market Leadership** — With 91% market share, the focus should shift toward deepening customer loyalty and product/service quality to prevent competitive erosion.
